Dear Stripe team,

I'm applying for the Forward Deployed AI Accelerator role because when I read the listing, I immediately recognized the type of work I've been doing at Loop Earplugs since 2024. Stripe describes FDAs embedded with about 20 marketers, building tools alongside them and moving them from awareness to self-sufficiency. At Loop, I've run a similar arc across six teams: map the real work, build on live deliverables, encode what works into reusable substrate, transfer ownership, move on. What started as a months-long embedding has now become a scalable system I can now apply to almost any team.

Why Stripe? AI is reshaping how work gets done and how new businesses get built. Smaller teams, new operating models, agents becoming co-founders, etc… I want to work at the financial infrastructure layer underneath that shift. The job opening also echoes what I've been doing at Loop: adoption and automation are not separate tracks. The person teaching marketers to rethink their work should also be the person helping them building their tools..

My core learning at Loop: adoption starts with navigation. AI isn't software to command. It's intelligence; the first kind you can use as a tool and work with as a collaborator, often both at once. You don't command something that interprets you back. You navigate it which fundamentally changes ‘what’ work is.

Because the artifact that used to be the end of the process, like a a paid social brief, a landing page, a campaign plan; etc … has now become the starting point for an agent, a Skill, or a workflow. The thing you used to finish for the team is now the brief you give to the system. Which means everyone now has to answer a question most have never had to ask: how do I actually want AI to transform how I work? The hard part here isn't technical; it's helping them answer that. My job is to walk them through it, then make sure they're self-sufficient once they’ve answered it.

At Loop

Loop gave me a compressed, high-feedback environment to prove this: 230 people, fast-moving, global, with marketing as the engine of the business. That's why I went to marketing, and embedded myself in Studio first to scale creative output without losing craft. I joined reviews, scanned briefs for AI angles, changed the mental model first, and built the tooling underneath.

Studio is now an AI-first creative operation: a range of Claude tone-of-voice Skills across paid social, CRM, and marketplaces compresses the copy workload. A custom image and video platform I built handles the production. The result: 90% of 500 monthly ads use AI, campaigns ship 2–3× faster than external agencies, and we consistently beat ROAS benchmarks. All of it driven autonomously by the Studio team.

This same operating system then traveled to Performance Marketing and Localization. For Performance, I built a briefing agent that synthesizes past campaigns, ad performance, and competitive data for the Creative Strategists, replacing what used to be days of manual research per cycle. For Localization, an end-to-end pipeline that transcribes, translates, and dubs top-performing UGC into new markets in hours; work that previously needed agency handoffs across 30+ markets. In both cases I built the first version with the team, then taught them to extend it themselves. Performance now adds its own data sources to the briefing agent. Localization shapes the dubbing pipeline around its own reviewer workflow. The work I started is the work they own now.

The throughline across all of it: teaching people how to encode their way of working into substrate any LLM can inherit. The experience, the judgment, the evaluation criteria, captured in a format the models can read. Without that layer, nothing carries forward; models change every quarter, and the work has to start from scratch. With it, the work compounds without me, because the real thing being taught isn't the tools. It's the instinct to recognize when something in the work should be captured in the first place. Once a team has that instinct, they don't need me to point at the next opportunity. They extend their own tools, teach their peers, and the next team I embed with starts further along than the last. The whole flywheel is laid out at shards-cyan.vercel.app/ai-operator (access: Dromineer).
