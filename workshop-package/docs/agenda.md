# Workshop · run of show

A two-hour shape, delivered in person or remote. Times are guidelines,
not a script. The session lands when the team leaves with one Skill
they can use the next day.

## Pre-work (sent the week before)

Two short asks. Five minutes each.

1. **One workflow you do often.** Something repetitive enough that you
   could explain it to a new colleague in five minutes. Examples: brief
   a designer, audit a video edit, format an interview, draft alt copy
   for a campaign, cut a long-form into social.
2. **Three good past examples** of that workflow's output. The version
   you would point a new hire at and say "this is what good looks like."

Send the two answers as a short Slack/email reply. Do not over-prepare.

## The session

| Time | Beat | Frame |
|------|------|-------|
| 0:00–0:10 | Welcome and reframe | 00 · Hero, 01 · The reframe |
| 0:10–0:25 | What changed | 02 · Collapse, 03 · Flywheel |
| 0:25–0:40 | Navigate · live demo | 04 · Navigate |
| 0:40–0:55 | Encode · what a Skill is | 05 · Encode |
| 0:55–1:25 | **Build a Skill on the fly** | 06 · Encode live |
| 1:25–1:40 | What you can build on top | 07 · Build |
| 1:40–1:55 | The starter kit + second brain | 08 · Kit, 09 · Second brain |
| 1:55–2:00 | Sign-off and next steps | 10 · Closer |

The 30-minute block on Encode Live is the deliverable. Everything
upstream of it is framing. Everything downstream is "what becomes
possible after this."

## What to bring

- The two pre-work answers (one workflow, three examples).
- A laptop with Claude open, ideally with Skills enabled.
- A short, real task in the same shape as the workflow you picked, so
  you can test the Skill at step three of the recipe.

## How to run the live build

Use `docs/build-a-skill.md` as the working sheet. Walk the room through
the four steps in parallel, with everyone on their own workflow. Pause
for share-outs after step two (the draft Skill) and step three (the
first run on real work).

This is not a demo. It is a working session. The room writes; you
co-pilot.

## What success looks like

- Every participant leaves with a draft Skill, even if rough.
- At least three of the room's drafts get used the same week.
- One of them gets shared with the team.
- Two weeks later, the question shifts from "should we use AI?" to
  "what should we encode next?"

## What to avoid

- Generic AI demos. The room has seen them.
- Pure AI-generation showpieces. The brief is process, not output.
- Promising "we'll fix the bad edit with AI." We will not. The honest
  message is the credible one.
- Certificates, completion tracking, attendance theatre. This is a
  flywheel, not a training program.

## Follow-up · two weeks later

Forty-five minutes. Each participant brings:

- The Skill they ran on real work
- Where it nailed it
- Where it needed an extra rule or example
- Whether they shared it with anyone

This is the moment the substrate compounds. The follow-up is the
program; the workshop is the kickoff.
