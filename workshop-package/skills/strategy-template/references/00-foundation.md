# <Your Team> · Foundation

Loaded when a task needs the worldview behind the strategy. The arguments
that justify the spine, not the spine itself. If a stakeholder pushes
back on the framing, this is where the answers live.

## The shift you are responding to

One or two paragraphs naming the change in the world (or the market, or
the team's situation) that makes the strategy necessary now. What stopped
working. What started working. Why the old playbook is no longer the
right one.

## What you believe that the room often does not

Three to five short claims, one per paragraph. Each one is a load-bearing
belief: if it turns out wrong, the strategy needs revising. Phrase them
as declarative statements, not slogans.

1. **<Claim one>.** Why you believe it. The evidence in your own work.
2. **<Claim two>.** Why you believe it.
3. **<Claim three>.** Why you believe it.
4. **<Claim four>.** Why you believe it.

## What is uniquely yours

The piece of the work that no one else can copy because it grew out of
your team's actual experience. This is the moat. Be specific about what
it is, where it lives, and how it shows up in the output.

## What is not yours

The piece you cede to others. Naming this explicitly keeps the offering
clean. If the room is already building X, you do not drift into X. You
own Y, and you name the seam where the two meet.

## Vocabulary

A short glossary of the terms you use that need to mean something
specific. Each entry is one or two sentences. Avoid abstract jargon; use
the words the team already says out loud.

- **<Term one>**: <one-sentence definition>.
- **<Term two>**: <one-sentence definition>.
- **<Term three>**: <one-sentence definition>.

## Failure modes

What happens when the strategy is misapplied. Two or three concrete
patterns. Naming them up front makes them easier to spot in early drafts.

- **<Failure mode one>**: <how it shows up, what to do instead>.
- **<Failure mode two>**: <how it shows up, what to do instead>.
- **<Failure mode three>**: <how it shows up, what to do instead>.
