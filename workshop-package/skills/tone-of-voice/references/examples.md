# Examples · before and after

Calibration set. Concrete rewrites that show what each voice rule does
in practice. When the model is starting fresh on a draft, load this
file and pattern-match against the closest example.

## Em dashes

**Before**
> The workshop wasn't a lecture — it was a working session — and that
> changed how the team showed up.

**After**
> The workshop was a working session, not a lecture. That changed how
> the team showed up.

## Filler authority words

**Before**
> Importantly, this is fundamentally a deployment problem, not a model
> problem. It's worth noting that the labs have said so themselves.

**After**
> This is a deployment problem, not a model problem. The labs have said
> so themselves.

## Reflex rule-of-three

**Before**
> The Skill is fast, accurate, and consistent.

**After**
> The Skill produces the same answer twice in a row. That alone is most
> of the point.

(Two of the three items were padding. The third one, "consistent",
carried the whole claim. Lead with it; cut the rest.)

## Balanced "X is not Y, it is Z"

**Before**
> AI is not a magic fix, it is a speed layer.

**After**
> AI is a speed layer. Treat it like one.

(The negation eats half the sentence and adds nothing. The positive
claim does the work alone.)

## Tagline-shaped headers

**Before**
> ## Where the magic lives

**After**
> ## What the Skill does

## Hedge stripping

**Before**
> This might potentially be a useful approach for teams that could
> benefit from a more structured workflow.

**After**
> Try this with any team that has more than three repeating workflows.

## Section opener (long-form)

**Before**
> In today's rapidly evolving landscape of artificial intelligence,
> organizations are increasingly seeking ways to leverage these
> powerful technologies to drive meaningful business outcomes.

**After**
> Pure AI generation has plateaued. The gains are around the process
> now, not the output.

## Email opener

**Before**
> I hope this finds you well! I wanted to circle back about the workshop
> and share some thoughts on how we might approach things going forward.

**After**
> Quick follow-up on the workshop. Two things to land before next week.

## Social post

**Before**
> Excited to share that we just shipped a new Skill that helps teams
> accelerate their content workflow! 🚀 Curious to hear what you think.

**After**
> New Skill: drafts social posts in our voice from a one-line brief.
> Cut the writing time from forty minutes to four. Try it; tell me
> where it gets it wrong.

## Description / abstract

**Before**
> This presentation explores the transformative potential of AI within
> creative production environments, examining various ways teams can
> leverage these emerging technologies.

**After**
> A workshop on using AI inside the creative process. Not the output.
> Three motions: navigate, encode, build. Hands-on from minute fifteen.

## Headline

**Before**
> Unlocking the Power of AI for Modern Creative Teams

**After**
> A speed layer on the creative process.
