04 · NAVIGATE

Treat it as intelligence, not software.

Sometimes you use AI as a tool. Sometimes you work with it as a collaborator. The shift is choosing which one the task needs.

← AS A TOOL

Deterministic enough to automate.

Use it for the routine: transcripts, decks, alt copy, briefing docs, batch renames, format conversions. Lock the freedom band down.

  • Clear input, clear expected output
  • Same task, many times
  • Quality measured by accuracy, not taste
AS A COLLABORATOR →

Interpretive enough to think with.

Brainstorm, critique, draft alternatives, surface counter-arguments. Open the freedom band up. Read the output as a colleague’s draft, not a finished answer.

  • Ambiguous input, multiple good outputs
  • One-off or shaping work
  • Quality measured by judgment, not pattern match

The mistake is asking it to make a bad edit good. AI is a speed layer, not a salvage operation.

05 · ENCODE

A Skill is one file that hands the model your judgment.

A text file with instructions and reference materials that encodes the way you think about a specific kind of work. The model reads it the way a new colleague reads a notebook: rules, examples, and taste, then applies them.

Trigger When this Skill applies

The shape of the request that calls it: brief docs, video transcripts, asset audits, supplier briefs.

Rules + Examples How the work gets done well

The taste, the constraints, the calls only seniors make. With concrete examples of good and bad outcomes.

References What it can read from

Brand systems, reference documents, prior good work. Loaded only when needed (progressive disclosure).

Loops Who confirms, who decides

Hand-off back to a person at the right moment. Not every step is the model’s call to make.

SKILL.md
--- name: Tone of Voice description: When the work is long-form copy, social posts, an interview write-up… --- # Tone of Voice ## Voice rules - no em dashes - declarative sentences - no forced rule-of-three ## What good looks like See references/examples.md ## Routing | Trigger | Reference |
06 · ENCODE LIVE

Build a Skill on the fly. Four steps.

Pick one workflow you do often. Walk it through these four moves and you have a draft Skill the team can inherit by the end of the workshop.

01 PICK

A workflow that repeats.

Something you do at least once a week and could explain to a new colleague in five minutes. Audit a video. Brief a designer. Format an interview.

02 NARRATE

Explain it out loud.

Drop three good past examples plus your spoken explanation into Claude. Use /skill-creator or just paste them in. Ask it to draft the Skill back.

03 RUN

Test on real work.

Try the Skill on a fresh task in the same shape. The first run will be wrong in interesting ways. Note where, then add the missing rule or example.

04 SHARE

Hand it to the team.

Export the bundle. Drop it into a shared folder, or upload to Claude.ai. The next person doesn’t start from zero.

The first Skill takes the longest. Every Skill after that compounds.

07 · BUILD

A Skill becomes a tool.

Once the substrate is there, a workflow stops being a chat and becomes a thing the team can call. Three shapes worth knowing for video and creative production.

VIDEO → PAGE

Landing page from a recorded interview.

Transcribe the interview, run the Skill that knows your tone and structure, draft a single-page site with quotes, key moments, and embedded clips. The substrate is the brand voice; the tool is the page generator.

Video→ Transcript→ Skill→ HTML
EDIT ASSIST

Cut suggestions, not cuts.

Send the transcript and the brief to a Skill that knows what a good edit looks like for the format. Get back marked-up suggestions, ranked. The editor still cuts. The thinking shortcut is the gain.

Transcript→ Brief→ Skill→ Marked timeline
REPURPOSE

One piece, five formats.

A long-form video becomes social cuts, an explainer page, a press one-pager, a recap newsletter. Same source, the Skills handle the format-specific judgment. The interface just collects them.

Source→ Skills→ Format set
08 · STARTER KIT

What you just unpacked. Use it as a seed.

A second-brain starter. Not a finished system. Each piece below is a template you adapt to how your team actually works.

    This deck. The narrative and the run-of-show in one self-contained file. Open it offline. Project it. Edit it.

  • skills/strategy-template/

    Skeleton for your own strategy Skill. A SKILL.md plus references/, ready to fill with your team’s judgment.

  • skills/tone-of-voice/

    A worked example. What a tone-of-voice Skill looks like once it’s done. Copy the shape, swap the content.

  • skills/examples/*.skill

    Installable bundles. Frontend Design, Plain English, Creating Presentations, AI Adoption. Drop into Claude.ai or ~/.claude/skills/.

  • docs/agenda.md

    The session shape. A run-of-show you can adapt. Where to spend time, where to compress.

  • docs/build-a-skill.md

    The tangible exercise. The four-step recipe in detail, with what to bring, where it goes wrong, how to fix it.

Drop the whole folder into Claude Cowork. Ask it to “set me up” and it will install the Skills, open the deck, and walk you through the docs.
